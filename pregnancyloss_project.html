The Lasting Economic Impact of Pregnancy Loss

This project explores how pregnancy loss is associated with changes in women's employment and earnings over time. Using data from the UK Office for National Statistics (ONS), the analysis examines whether adverse pregnancy events correspond with measurable disruptions in labour market outcomes.

ðŸ”Ž Project Overview

Pregnancy loss can have profound emotional and physical effects. However, its economic consequences are less frequently examined. This dashboard investigates two key questions:

  • How does pregnancy loss affect women's earnings?
  • Are women less likely to be employed following a loss?

Understanding these patterns may help inform workplace policy, economic support programmes, and advocacy efforts focused on gender equality, women's reproductive rights, and employee wellbeing.

ðŸ§¹ Data Source and Preparation

Data for this project was obtained from the Office for National Statistics (ONS), specifically:

"The impact of adverse pregnancy events on monthly employee earnings and employment, England (2014â€“2022)."

The dataset provides modelled estimates of earnings and employment probability relative to pre-event baselines.

Preparation steps included:

  • Unioning multiple ONS tables into a single analytical dataset
  • Standardising field names for clarity
  • Creating calculated fields to categorise event types
  • Structuring time relative to the pregnancy event for longitudinal analysis

All data used is aggregated and anonymised.
