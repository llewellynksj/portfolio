The Lasting Economic Impact of Pregnancy Loss

This project explores how pregnancy loss is associated with changes in women’s employment and earnings over time. Using data from the UK Office for National Statistics (ONS), it examines whether adverse pregnancy events correspond with measurable disruptions in labour market outcomes.

Project Overview

Pregnancy loss can have profound emotional and physical effects. However, its economic consequences are less frequently examined. This analysis focuses on two key questions:

  • How does pregnancy loss affect women’s earnings?
  • Are women less likely to be employed following a loss?

Understanding these patterns may help inform workplace policy, economic support programmes, and advocacy efforts focused on gender equality and employee wellbeing.

Interactive Dashboard

Key Insights

  • Employment declines immediately following pregnancy loss, but generally recovers within several quarters.
  • Earnings fall after pregnancy loss and remain below baseline for longer, even after many women return to work.
  • This suggests that returning to employment does not necessarily restore prior earning trajectories.
  • Patterns are consistent across loss categories, though the magnitude varies.

Understanding the Charts

Chart 1: Earnings over time

This chart shows how women’s average monthly earnings change in the months before and after pregnancy loss. Earnings are stable prior to the event, drop sharply afterwards, and recover gradually — though not always fully.

Each line represents the modelled change in average monthly earnings (£) compared to a baseline period before the pregnancy event.

Data Source & Preparation

Data was obtained from the Office for National Statistics (ONS), covering England from 2014–2022.

  • Unioned multiple ONS tables
  • Standardised variables
  • Created event-relative time measures
  • Ensured anonymised, aggregated outputs

Why This Matters

Although many women return to work following pregnancy loss, reduced earnings often persist. This highlights the importance of supportive workplace policies and financial protections.
